About Me

WHAT MY LINKEDIN DOESN'T SAY

My current profession is front end web development but that is only a part of my web development interests. Some of my experiences included but not limited to:

  • Collaborating with designers to bring pixel-perfect designs to life
  • Designing the database schema for migrating external content into
  • Providing a way to interact with data and information in a meaningful way
  • Creating user stories of the different audiences who visits the website
  • Working with stakeholders to make sure the product meets and/or exceeds expectations

 

THE BEGINNING

I started web development since 2010 and it was purely by chance. Before that point, I actually thought web development was useless and it was just HTML and making things look nice. Boy, was I glad to be wrong.

Senior year of undergrad, I was brought on as an intern in a biology lab to write up scripts since I had a coding background and was majoring in bioinformatics. Sometime through my intership, the lab's webmaster was overwhelmed in projects and sent some tasks my way. Being the intern, I was like "sure, why not, just some HTML". Nope, it was more than that. Web development was completely not what I expected. In that summer alone I was already doing things with the LAMP stack.

But even with that exposure to web development, I was wrapping up my internship and ready to move on to something that hopefully had something to do my major, probably even graduate school. But as chance would have it (the chance I mentioned earlier), the webmaster himself was moving on to better things thus the position opened up. My supervisor believed in me, pushed me to apply, and I did. The rest was history.
